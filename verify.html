Get verified

Earn the Trusted by MyBI badge

Cross-signing lets users trust your plugin without trusting you blindly. You sign your plugin with your key; MyBI countersigns your public key. No private key is ever shared — in either direction.

Trust tiers

Three levels of trust

First-party MyBI

Built and signed by MyBI with the first-party key. The blocks that ship inside the app.

Third-party Trusted by MyBI

Built by you, signed with your key, and countersigned by MyBI after review. Users see a verified badge.

Community community

Self-signed and installable by anyone. Genuine and untampered — just not reviewed or countersigned by MyBI.

How cross-signing works

Two studios, one public-key handshake

Publisher Studio is yours. Signer Studio is MyBI's. Only public keys and signed artifacts cross between them — secrets stay put.

  Publisher Studio · you
Generate your keypair
🔒 private key — never leaves this machine
Sign your plugin with it
Export public key + signed plugin
public key (leak-safe) countersigned cert
  Signer Studio · MyBI
Review the publisher
Countersign the public key
🔑 first-party seed — in memory only
Issue “Trusted by MyBI” cert

Why this is safe. Your private signing key never leaves Publisher Studio, and the MyBI first-party seed never leaves Signer Studio. MyBI only signs your public key — a leak-safe token. The result: anyone can verify that your plugin was signed by you and that MyBI vouched for your key, without either side ever exposing a secret.

What gets verified

A reproducible signature

Signatures are Ed25519 over a canonical payload — verifiable by anyone, exactly what the app checks on install.

# canonical signing payload (Ed25519)
mybi-plugin-v1
acme.sales-funnel
1.0.0
sha256(manifest.json)
sha256(index.js)

# the app verifies, on install:
1) plugin signature      → signed by the publisher's key
2) publisher countersign  → that key vouched for by MyBI
⇒ Trusted by MyBI ✓
Step by step

Getting your plugin verified

  1. Build your pluginFollow the build guide — manifest, declared permissions, sandbox-ready.
  2. Generate a keypair in Publisher StudioAn Ed25519 keypair is created locally; the private key never leaves your machine.
  3. Sign your pluginPublisher Studio signs the canonical payload with your private key.
  4. Submit your public key for reviewSend MyBI your public key and plugin details — never a secret.
  5. MyBI countersignsAfter review, Signer Studio countersigns your public key with the first-party seed and issues a certificate.
  6. Ship as Trusted by MyBIPublish the signed plugin + certificate. The app shows the verified badge; revocation stays possible if ever needed.
